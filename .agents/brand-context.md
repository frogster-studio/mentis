# Brand Context

Summary of the founder's notes (translated from French). This captures the vision, motivation, and product direction behind the project.

## Vision

Make general knowledge ("culture générale") modern and genuinely fun — a contemporary take on the spirit of TV quiz shows like *Les 12 coups de midi*. The founder doesn't watch the show itself, but loves learning things, and believes there is a real gap in the market for making general knowledge cool.

## The Problem

- The world is getting "dumber": people consume content passively but rarely nurture their curiosity.
- Existing general-knowledge formats (e.g. Trivial Pursuit) are deadly boring — nobody wants to play them. Yet when it's well presented, everyone enjoys it.
- People shouldn't be mere consumers; they should be curious and proactive in order to be interesting.

## The Belief

- Learning small things, discovering anecdotes, and understanding the world makes you more interesting — it's what differentiates us from machines.
- Curiosity makes us smarter, more socially engaging and engaged, and open to the world, instead of retreating into a dangerous — even destructive — echo chamber.
- It starts small: little questions and quizzes that open you up to topics you don't usually talk about.
- **"The brain needs a good feed too."** (plays on "feed" as both nourishment and content feed — candidate tagline)

## The Products

### 1. General-knowledge app (the core project)

A long-standing ambition: build an app for managing/engaging with general knowledge. There is a genuine opportunity to turn general knowledge into something people actively want to do. Public figures pushing this space (e.g. Aurélien from *Les 12 coups de midi*, ~2010s era) show there's momentum. Communication and marketing around it can stay fairly simple, so this is the direction to commit to.

### 2. Weekly podcast

- Anecdote-driven, once a week, 40 minutes to 1 hour.
- Format: standalone quizzes with no strong link between them — a question, the possible answers, then the explanation.
- Keep it simple: don't overthink it, but make something clean and polished.

## Tone & Positioning

- Modern, fun, accessible — the opposite of dusty trivia.
- Low-friction curiosity: small questions as a gateway to unfamiliar topics.
- Aimed at people who consume passively today but could become curious and proactive learners.
